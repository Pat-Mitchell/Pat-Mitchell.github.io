Linear Algebra

 While reading Prime Obsession: Bernhard Riemann and the Greatest Unsolved Problem in Mathematics by John Derbyshire, I noticed that quaternions were a frequently mentioned topic and I had no prior knowledge of what they were. I could only surmise that it was an import topic for mathematics and I wanted to know what they were.

 A little research led to linear algebra and then Ivan Savov's No Bullshit Guide to Linear Algebra. It's an excellent book on the subject that's easy to digest and intended for the more casual audience. The last few chapters of the book are dedicated to applications of linear algebra like quantum mechanics, probability theory, computer graphics.
