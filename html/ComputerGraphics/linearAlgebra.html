I could only surmise that it was an import topic for mathematics and I wanted to know what they were.

 A little research led to linear algebra and then Ivan Savov's No Bullshit Guide to Linear Algebra. It's an excellent book on the subject that's easy to digest and intended for the more casual audience. The last few chapters of the book are dedicated to applications of linear algebra like quantum mechanics, probability theory, and computer graphics.

Quaternions

 Quaternions are complex numbers composed of vector (imaginary) and scalar (real) component and are used to represent either a direction or point in 3d and 2d space. They are expressed as:

  v=(x,y,w)

Where the w component is the real part typically holding the value of 0 for a direction and 1 for a position in space. A quaternion used in 3d space is then represented as:

  v=(x,y,z,w)
